Hello. I'm a second year PhD student at the University of Surrey UK, working in the field of self-supervised machine learning for video understanding in collaboration with the British Film Institute. 

What does that actually mean? Well, whereas much of video understanding in ML focuses on what is happening within a series a frames, I am interested in the contextual information surrounding the actions and how this contextual information can change what the actions mean in context to other actions in the same video. This contextual information could include the positioning of actors, movement of the camera, costume, colour grading, environments etc. In film theory the collection of these devices are known as "cinematic language".

Why is this useful? Commercially, my research has applications in video recomendation. From an education perspective, you could build a system to track the development of cinematic techniques throughout history (something I am working on with the BFI). But what I am most interested in is how this contextual information changes the way that we perceive the actor or actions within a scene and how well ML can model these intertextual relationships. From a philosophical perspective, you could say that my research explores whether it is possible to model semiotic and symbolic relationships and how we can train Artificial Agents to learn from media without inheriting harmful biases introduced via intertextual information.
